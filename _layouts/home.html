Overview

Our aim is to reduce the drooling symptoms of people with Parkinson's throught cueing. Sialorrhoea, also termed drooling or ptyalism, is a common symptom of Parkinson’s, affecting up to 80% of people. Cue Band, funded by Parkinson's UK, is develping cueing devices to diminish the drooling symptoms.

Technology

Cue Band in comprised of an mobile application and wristaband wearable allowing the following:

  • Create a 7-day schedule of cueing of times
  • Start and stop cueing at any moment
  • Define intensity of the cueing
  • Decide where to receive the cueing - mobile or band
  • Set type of cueing - mobile vibration, audio or band vibration
  • Use the band as a standalone to receive the cueing
  • Create daily self-reports to register your drooling symptoms

Study

We will take a mixed-methods approach, our recruitment strategy and study design are illustrated in figure 1. Using quantitative methods to explore the potential effects of the cueing method (i.e. AxLE-CUE wearable band vs. smartphone only) on the perceived drooling severity and frequency. We will conduct a comparison study with the current state of the art assistive device – smartphone app providing notifications (audio and/or vibrations) to prompt for swallowing. It is estimated there are 145,000 PwP in the UK, of nearly 80% experience symptoms of oropharyngeal dysphagia, giving a population of 118,900. We will recruit 300 PwP (95%, CI=5.65%) to participate in our eight-week cueing method intervention study, we are using a within-subjects design. We will capture formal assessments of the individuals symptoms before and after each intervention method. Formal Assessments will comprise of questions from the ROMP-Saliva [3], UPDRS 2.2 subset for saliva [2] and NMSQ [1]. During this time, participants will be asked to maintain a daily diary using the tools provided through our mobile app. They will self-report on their swallowing severity, frequency and duration; as well as provide any additional comments or reflections on their experiences that day. Prior to receiving the interventions, participants will complete a two-week baseline during our on boarding phase, whereby they complete the daily diary and self-reporting without any intervention. All 3000 participants will be posted a free AxLE-CUE device which they can keep and continue to use beyond the study. Group C, the 2700 PwP will only be providing anonymous usage data (i.e. cueing schedule, physical activity 4 and sleep behaviours), they will also have the option to provide feedback and suggestions through the mobile app. For our 300 PwP involved in the cueing method intervention study (Groups A & B). Each participant of Groups A & B will experience both interventions (AxLECUE wearable and smartphone only); however, the intervention order will be counter-balanced to avoid learning effects or additional bias. Intervention order will be based on a randomised allocation. They will be asked to use the intervention for a period of three weeks, while maintaining the daily diaries and selfreporting. After which point, participants will receive the alternative intervention for three weeks. Following the intervention phase of our study, participants will be asked to complete a questionnaire about their experience and preferences regarding both interfaces (AxLE-CUE wearable and smartphone only). Participants will be allowed to retain the AxLE-CUE wearable beyond our evaluation; however, we will disable any data sharing with the research team. As part of the debrief phase we will assist the participants in setting up their preferred prompting method (AxLE-CUE wearable or smartphone only), or disabling and removing the mobile applications if they no long wish to use them. Through qualitative methods, we hope understand daily experiences or PwP with symptoms of drooling and their opinions on the acceptability and feasibility of personal assistive technologies to cue for swallowing. We will aim to recruit 30 participants from the intervention study (Groups A & B) to participate in a subsequent semi-structured interview (via Telephone or Online audio/video call).
